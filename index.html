First-Author Publications

Expanded entries below list the full set of first-author peer-reviewed publications from the CV. The abstract text is paraphrased into concise summaries for readability.

2026
Can AI Match Human Experts? Evaluating LLM-Generated Feedback on Resident Scholarly Projects
medRxiv (preprint)
van Allen, Z., Forgues-Martel, S., Venables, M. J., Ghanney, Y., Villeneuve, A., Dongmo, J., Ahmed, M., Archibald, D., and Jolin-Dahel, K.
Open preprint
Abstract summary

Assesses whether LLM-generated feedback on resident scholarly work can approach expert human feedback in quality, usefulness, and consistency under blinded evaluation conditions.

2025
Prospective Classification of Functional Dependence: Insights from Machine Learning and the Canadian Longitudinal Study on Aging
Physiotherapy Canada
Open paper
Abstract summary

Uses machine learning on longitudinal aging data to prospectively classify functional dependence and identify a compact set of predictors that may support earlier risk stratification in older adults.

2024
Prestroke Physical Activity Matters for Functional Limitations: A Longitudinal Case-Control Study of 12,860 Participants
Physical Therapy
Open paper
Abstract summary

Shows that people who were more physically active before stroke had lower odds of later functional limitations, highlighting prestroke activity as a meaningful protective factor in recovery-related outcomes.

2024
A Multiple Behaviour Temporal Network Analysis for Health Behaviours During COVID-19
British Journal of Health Psychology
Open paper
Abstract summary

Models several health behaviours as a temporal network during the pandemic to examine how behaviour changes co-occurred and influenced one another over time rather than treating each behaviour in isolation.

2023
Clustering of Health Behaviors in Canadians: A Multiple Behavior Analysis of Data from the Canadian Longitudinal Study on Aging
Annals of Behavioral Medicine
Open paper
Abstract summary

Identifies distinct health behaviour profiles in a large Canadian aging cohort and shows how combinations of behaviours, not just single behaviours, help characterize health risk patterns across the population.

2021
Clustering of Unhealthy Behaviors: Protocol for a Multiple Behavior Analysis of Data from the Canadian Longitudinal Study on Aging
JMIR Research Protocols
Open paper
Abstract summary

Outlines the rationale, variables, and analytic plan for studying how unhealthy behaviours cluster together in the CLSA, providing a methodological blueprint for the later empirical multiple-behaviour analyses.

2021
Enacted Extraversion as a Well-Being Enhancing Strategy in Everyday Life: Testing Across Three, Week-Long Interventions
Collabra: Psychology
Open paper
Abstract summary

Tests whether deliberately acting more extraverted in daily life improves well-being across repeated week-long interventions, showing benefits for positive affect while also clarifying the boundaries of the effect.

2021
Barriers to and Enablers of Attendance at Diabetic Retinopathy Screening Experienced by Immigrants to Canada From Multiple Cultural and Linguistic Minority Groups
Diabetic Medicine
Open paper
Abstract summary

Explores why immigrant patients may or may not attend diabetic retinopathy screening, highlighting the role of language, logistics, system navigation, trust, and culturally responsive care.

2018
Testing Trait-State Isomorphism in a New Domain: An Exploratory Manipulation of Openness to Experience
Frontiers in Psychology
Open paper
Abstract summary

Examines whether state-level changes in openness-related behaviour mirror the broader personality trait, using an exploratory manipulation to test trait-state isomorphism in a new domain.
